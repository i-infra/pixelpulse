1. Setup

Requires: a DMM (one voltage reading) and one precision resistor (2.5–100 Ω, value known to 1% or better).

Close other pixelpulse tabs during calibration — they will fight over the device. The device's stored calibration is reset at the start of this procedure.

2. Voltage measurement — automatic

Leave both channel pins unconnected. The inputs are switched internally to GND and to the 2.5 V rail (no current flows in HI_Z, so the 50 Ω path drops nothing).

3. Voltage source — automatic

Each channel sources 0 V and 2.5 V unloaded and reads back its own output through the calibrated measure path.

4. Current zero

Confirm nothing is connected to the CH A and CH B pins, then capture the open-circuit current offsets.

5. Channel A current

  1. Connect the precision resistor between the CH A pin and the 2.5 V pin.

The wizard drives voltages/currents through the resistor; the true current is computed from the calibrated pin voltage and the resistor value.

6. Channel B current

  1. Move the resistor to between the CH B pin and the 2.5 V pin.

7. Review & write

Gains should be near 1.0 and offsets small; suspect rows are highlighted.
